Tech Quarterly Earnings Reports
Meta, Google, and Amazon all posted strong Q2 2025 earnings, driven by AI and ad growth, with Meta and Amazon showing double-digit revenue gains and Google leading in ad and cloud expansion.

Meta Q2 2025 Highlights

  • Revenue up 22% to $47.5B; net income up 36% to $18.3B—both above expectations.
  • Daily active users rose 6% to 3.48B.
  • Ads drove 21% revenue growth, boosted by new AI tools used by 2M+ advertisers.
  • Big focus on AI: new Superintelligence Lab, heavy investment in tech and talent.
  • Reality Labs (smart glasses, VR) up 5%, but still not profitable.
  • Capex raised to fund AI and data center growth.
  • Returned $11B to shareholders.
  • Q3 outlook remains strong with ad and AI growth continuing.

Google (Alphabet) Q2 2025 Highlights

  • Revenue up 14% to $96.4B; net income up 19% to $28.2B; EPS rose 22% to $2.31, all ahead of expectations.
  • Strong growth across core businesses: Search, YouTube, subscriptions, and Cloud.
  • Google Cloud revenue surged 32% to $13.6B; profitability more than doubled.
  • AI at the center: new AI-powered search features, YouTube Shorts growth, launch of “Google AI Ultra” subscription.
  • Capital expenditures soared 70% to $22.5B, guidance for 2025 CapEx raised to $85B, focused on AI and cloud infrastructure.
  • Outlook positive, with growing demand for AI and cloud services.

Amazon Q2 2025 Highlights

  • Revenue up 13% to $167.7B; net income up 35% to $18.2B; EPS $1.68, all beating forecasts.
  • North America sales rose 11%; International up 16%; AWS (cloud) grew 18% to $30.9B.
  • Operating income jumped to $19.2B.
  • AI was a major focus, with new features in Alexa, shopping, and logistics.
  • Held biggest Prime Day ever; expanded fast delivery to more cities.
  • Launched tools for AI-powered shopping and increased robotics automation.
  • Guidance cautious but demand and innovation remain strong.
